We collected multiple timing samples to reduce measurement noise and observe latency distributions.

Using 100 runs allowed us to generate histograms that show clustering of access times for each memory level.

The histogram shows that DRAM accesses have significantly higher latency than cache accesses.

Although L1, L2, and L3 latencies overlap somewhat due to measurement overhead and CPU security mitigations, they still form distinguishable clusters.

Repeating measurements helps average out system noise, scheduling interference, and timing variance.
